# SQL Query Results Summary

This document highlights key findings derived from SQL analysis.  
All results were generated using structured queries against relational datasets.

---

## 1. Sales Performance Overview
**Question:**  
What is the overall sales performance across products and time?

**Approach:**  
Aggregated transaction data by date and product to analyze revenue patterns.

**Result:**  
Sales volume is unevenly distributed across products, with a small subset contributing a significant share of total revenue.

**Business Insight:**  
Revenue concentration suggests an opportunity to optimize pricing, promotions, or inventory for high-performing products.

---

## 2. Top Products by Revenue
**Question:**  
Which products generate the most revenue?

**Approach:**  
Grouped sales data by product and ranked results in descending order of total sales.

**Result:**  
A limited number of products consistently rank at the top by revenue.

**Business Insight:**  
These products should be prioritized for marketing, stock availability, and profitability analysis.

---

## 3. Customer Contribution Analysis
**Question:**  
How is revenue distributed across customers?

**Approach:**  
Aggregated total sales by customer and analyzed distribution patterns.

**Result:**  
A small group of customers contributes a disproportionate share of revenue.

**Business Insight:**  
High-value customers represent both growth opportunities and concentration risk.

---

## 4. Time-Based Trends
**Question:**  
How does sales performance change over time?

**Approach:**  
Analyzed sales aggregated by month and year.

**Result:**  
Sales exhibit clear seasonal and trend-based patterns.

**Business Insight:**  
Understanding seasonality supports better forecasting and planning.

---

## 5. Data Quality Observations
**Question:**  
Are there data quality issues affecting analysis?

**Approach:**  
Checked for missing values, invalid records, and inconsistencies.

**Result:**  
Some missing and inconsistent values were identified and handled during analysis.

**Business Insight:**  
Data validation is essential before drawing conclusions from analytical results.

---

This analysis demonstrates how SQL can be used to:
- Answer business-focused questions
- Reveal performance patterns
- Identify risks and opportunities
